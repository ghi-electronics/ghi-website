## The challenge

Smart manufacturing depends on getting reliable production data out of equipment that wasn't designed to give it up. Older machines speak their own protocols (or none at all); newer ones speak too many. 4i Platform needed a hardware base its engineers could deploy across diverse plant environments — metals, automotive, general manufacturing — without rebuilding firmware for every site.

## The solution

**4iDASH** collects data directly from production line machines and feeds it to 4i Platform's analytics layer. The same hardware lineage powers two adjacent products:

- **PLT (Production Line Tracking)** — continuously tracks position and status of heavy equipment like steel ladles inside metals plants.
- **WMS (Warehouse Management System)** — RFID-driven inventory and asset tracking, in real time from the warehouse floor.

All three are deployed across customer plants worldwide, generating the production data that drives Smart Manufacturing decisions.

## Why TinyCLR

SITCore modules give 4i Platform a single validated hardware base — CPU, memory, networking, and the TinyCLR runtime — that drops onto custom carrier boards designed for each product family. Application code is C#, debugged in Visual Studio, and the same firmware modules carry across 4iDASH, PLT, and WMS hardware.

The tradeoff that mattered: predictable supply for products that live inside customer plants for years, with the runtime and toolchain stable enough that engineering effort stays focused on the protocols and analytics — not on bringing up a microcontroller.
