## Forward compatibility is a design goal, not an afterthought

Picking a chipset for a product that ships for a decade is really picking a vendor. We design our families to evolve gracefully — so when a generation changes, your team doesn't start over.

<div className="feature-grid">
  <div className="feature-card">
    <div className="feature-card__icon">🏭</div>
    <div className="feature-card__title">Sourcing under our control</div>
    <div className="feature-card__body">Because we design and manufacture on the same floor, we control component selection and production schedules — the two things that determine long-term availability.</div>
  </div>
  <div className="feature-card">
    <div className="feature-card__icon">🔄</div>
    <div className="feature-card__title">Compatible upgrade paths</div>
    <div className="feature-card__body">When a platform evolves, we aim for drop-in or near-drop-in successors. When small changes are needed — pin assignments, mechanical fit, or software APIs — we keep the impact as low as we reasonably can.</div>
  </div>
  <div className="feature-card">
    <div className="feature-card__icon">📅</div>
    <div className="feature-card__title">Family-scale planning</div>
    <div className="feature-card__body">We architect product families to span generations. A board you choose today fits into a roadmap that already accounts for the next refresh and the one after.</div>
  </div>
  <div className="feature-card">
    <div className="feature-card__icon">🏆</div>
