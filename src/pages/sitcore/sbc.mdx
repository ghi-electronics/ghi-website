## When to choose Single Board Computers

<div className="decision-callout">
  <div className="decision-callout__yes">
    <h3>Pick an SBC when</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>You're prototyping a product, not designing custom hardware yet</li>
      <li>You want a maker form factor (Arduino, Pico, Feather, micro:bit)</li>
      <li>You want USB cable, Visual Studio, and code in 10 minutes</li>
      <li>You're building a small-run product where SBC unit cost works</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div className="decision-callout__no">
    <h3>Consider something else if</h3>

      <li>You're evaluating for a commercial project — <a href="/sitcore/dev/">Development Board</a></li>
      <li>You're going to volume production — <a href="/sitcore/som/">System on Module</a></li>
      <li>You're designing your own board — <a href="/sitcore/soc/">System on Chip</a></li>

## Maker-friendly form factors

The SBC category is designed for compatibility with the most-used maker boards on the market. That compatibility lets you prototype with thousands of available accessories — without giving up commercial-grade hardware.
