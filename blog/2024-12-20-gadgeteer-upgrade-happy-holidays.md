We discontinued the all-beloved Gadgeteer modules years ago, but something new is in the works — and it goes much further than Gadgeteer ever did.

{/* truncate */}

Meet **DUELink** — Dynamic Universal Expandable Link. Where Gadgeteer required a specific mainboard and a specific host language, DUELink works with anything: Arduino, Raspberry Pi, PC, phone, ESP32, micro:bit. It speaks over USB, SPI, I2C, and UART, and it supports Python, C#, JavaScript, and more.

The software infrastructure is in place — internal scripting engine and host libraries for Python, JavaScript, and .NET are ready. The BigBang module is the foundation: a versatile board that serves as the blueprint for the entire catalog.

DUELink is also compatible with the Adafruit STEMMA and SparkFun Qwiic ecosystems, and uses 5mm-pitch mounting holes — familiar territory for anyone who built with Gadgeteer.

A full launch announcement is coming. In the meantime, more details at [duelink.com](https://duelink.com).
